# Concept Mutation

## What a concept is

A **concept** is a named, possibly described thing — a piece of the observable world as a person perceives it. A concept has a title and a description. The description is free-form: it can be structured, semi-structured, unstructured text, or even media. There are no format requirements — only that the concept meaningfully represents something in the person's world.

## Why concepts are mutable

Understanding deepens over time — language sharpens, context shifts, new experience arrives. The concept evolves with the person. Its identity persists through change; its description may not.

This is different from judgments, which are immutable. A belief is a crystallization — fixed at the moment of reflection. A concept is the thing being observed — it can be refined without producing a new belief.

## The edit act

Editing a concept is a first-class act in Fractality — not a side effect of cognitive acts, but a distinct, subordinate action.

When a person edits a concept, the concept's description changes. Its identity — what makes it *this* concept and not another — remains. Every edit is recorded as an version in BB history.

## The identity boundary

Editing has a limit. At some point a concept has changed enough that it is no longer a refinement — it has become a different concept. Where that line falls is entirely the person's call.

> It is the natural cognitive capability of every person to distinguish when a concept has changed enough to become a new concept.

This is not a weakness in the framework — it reflects the personal nature of knowledge. No external rule can define when *your* understanding of *engine* has diverged enough to warrant a new concept. Only you can.

## What editing does not affect

Editing a concept does not change any judgment. Judgments reference concept identity — not a specific description. A judgment reflexed before an edit remains valid after it. Whether the edited concept still makes the judgment semantically sound is, again, the person's liability.
