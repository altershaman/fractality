# Philosophy

## How we gather knowledge

From before birth, a person continuously absorbs experience — sensation, language, instruction, reflection. This is not passive recording. Every piece of new information meets existing beliefs, gets interpreted, connected, abstracted. Over a lifetime this produces a vast personal ontology: a living structure of how things relate, constantly revised by cognitive work. We don't just know things — we build, refine, and sometimes demolish what we know.

## Why subjectivity demands communication

Because every person builds their ontology independently, knowledge is irreducibly subjective. No two people interpret the same experience identically. Yet we live in shared reality and need aligned understanding to act together. This creates a natural pressure to communicate — to share beliefs, expose contradictions, negotiate meaning, and reach consensus. Consensus doesn't dissolve subjectivity; it creates shared knowledge layered on top of personal belief, while each person's inner structure remains their own.

## Purpose of Fractality

Many frameworks have approached this space — formal ontologies (OWL, RDF), knowledge graphs, personal knowledge tools (Zettelkasten, Roam), epistemic logics — each capturing part of the picture. Fractality synthesizes what they leave incomplete: a minimal formal framework that honors the personal and bounded nature of knowledge, is precise enough for machines, and treats communication as a first-class act rather than an afterthought.
