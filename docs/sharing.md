# Knowledge sharing

## Why communication exists

Knowledge is irreducibly personal. Every person builds their own understanding independently — no two people interpret the same experience identically. Yet we live in shared reality and need aligned understanding to act together.

This creates a natural pressure to communicate: to share beliefs, expose contradictions, negotiate meaning, and reach consensus. The need to communicate is not social convention — subjectivity demands it.

## Dispense

A person shares their current BB (or part) state through the act of **dispense**. What is shared is the current set of beliefs — what the person holds true and false at this moment.

Dispense does not expose cognitive history. How a person arrived at their beliefs, what they once held and retracted, what they negated along the way — none of this is part of what is shared. The recipient receives judgments, not biography.

## Acquire

**Acquire** is the act of receiving another person's judgments. It is not automatic or passive — the acquiring person chooses what to incorporate. This choice is itself a cognitive act: the recipient reads the incoming beliefs and decides which to reflex into their own BB, under their own liability.

The incoming judgments do not arrive as foreign objects. Once acquired, they become the person's own — held, questioned, and potentially negated or retracted just like any other belief.

## What communication is not

Communication in Fractality is not a merge. Two BBs do not become one. There is no synchronization, no override, no automatic reconciliation of conflicting beliefs.

When persons disagree — when one holds `+j` and another holds `-j` — that contradiction is not resolved by the framework. It is a signal: there is cognitive work to be done. Discussion, new experience, further reflection. The resolution, if it comes, happens through new cognitive acts in each person's own BB.

This is not a limitation. It is the point. Knowledge is personal in origin. Even when shared, it remains held by persons — bounded, revisable, alive.
